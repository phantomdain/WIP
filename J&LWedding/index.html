We’d love for our guests to join us in Semi-Formal attire.

Semi-Formal Dress Code

For Men

  • Dress shirt with or without a tie, worn under a dark suit.
  • Barong Tagalog or an embellished Chinese-style shirt is also acceptable.
  • Dark-colored, tailored slacks are required; jeans and khakis are not allowed.
  • Dark leather shoes with matching socks.

For Women

  • Dresses like the “little black dress” are ideal, with formal fabrics (e.g., satin, taffeta).
  • Dress length may be slightly above the knee to ankle-length.
  • Stylish pantsuits with matching top and bottom are allowed.
  • No jeans, khakis, skinny pants, or tight clothing.
  • Dressy shoes with an appropriate heel height; no flats.
  • Conservative jewelry and optional accessories like a purse, scarf, or shawl.

Groom & Bride

The Groom

John started as a Management major but discovered his true calling as a Digital Designer and Front-end Developer. A bookworm at heart, he grew up devouring stories and now finds himself captivated by topics like self-development, finance, and business. When he’s not designing or reading, John is probably working on his latest Gunpla model. Fun fact? He’s an INFJ—the rarest of all personality types. And as for Lauren, he finds her “Pretty Annoying” with an emphasis on “Pretty.”

The Bride

Lauren, a Marketing major, took an unexpected yet successful turn into accounting and procurement. She's always had a passion for dance, even competing in dance contests since she was young. As an ESFJ, she's known for her caring nature, love for socializing, and her eagerness to lend a helping hand. She sees John as the complementary support for anything she might lack.

Story

Our Love Story

First Meet

December 18, 2006

It was a Monday in December when John, head of a college theater group, held a casting call for a portrayal of Rizal’s death. Lauren walked in, brought by a friend, and John immediately noticed her. She was already popular as a ballroom dancer and Dance Troupe member, but this moment was something different.

First Date

February 20, 2007

For Lauren’s birthday, John planned a special outing. They set off to Forest Park in Angono, Rizal, only to find it closed. Instead, they took a quiet nature walk, sharing laughs over fishballs and discovering how comfortable it was to just be with each other.

Making It Official

October 4, 2012

After four years of dating, John and Lauren made it official right before a church mission trip to Korea. Lauren, a bit nervous, joked about John meeting someone new there, but he assured her that she was the only one.

Engagement

August 3, 2019

Before a trip to Singapore and Lauren’s upcoming move to Qatar, John popped the question. With a long-distance chapter ahead, they wanted to make sure they had forever planned, no matter the distance.
